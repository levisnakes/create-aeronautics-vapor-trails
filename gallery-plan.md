# Gallery plan — screenshots/GIFs to capture

Gallery images drive Modrinth conversions; the first two slots are the thumbnail and
first-impression shots, so both are close-up propeller shots. Capture at 1080p+ with shaders
(complementary/BSL) if they run clean, otherwise high-render-distance vanilla at golden hour.

1. **[Thumbnail — GIF] Takeoff burst, close-up.** Camera 6–10 blocks from a large bearing
   propeller at ground level on sand or dry grass. Sequence: props spin up (startup puff) →
   dust ring blasts outward under the disc → ship lifts off through its own dust cloud.
   Low sun angle so the dust catches light. This is the money shot.

2. **[Image or GIF] Cruising blade-tip halo, close-up.** In flight alongside the engine
   nacelle, propeller filling ~40% of frame, blade-tip vapor ring clearly flickering around
   the disc, prop-wash cone trailing behind. Best just under the cloud layer (~y 180–190)
   where the humidity boost doubles the ring density. Sky background, no ground clutter.

3. **[Image] Full-ship beauty shot with wingtip trails.** Large airship in a shallow dive at
   speed, camera behind and slightly above: both wingtip streaks visible converging behind
   the hull, cloud deck in the background. Shows scale + the signature trail effect.

4. **[GIF] Cloud punch.** Ship climbing vertically through the cloud layer: capture the mist
   ring bursting outward as the hull pierces y≈192. Frame the ring against the flat cloud
   deck so the circle reads clearly.

5. **[Image] Water wake.** Flying boat or low-flying hull skimming/moving through open water
   at speed: V-shaped foam arms and stern churn visible, ideally from 30–40 blocks up and
   behind at ~30° so the V geometry is obvious.

6. **[Image] Engine smoke + hover dust combo.** Ship hovering low over a sandy shore at dawn:
   grey engine smoke rising from the nacelles, dust drifting out from under the hull, spray
   ring where one prop overhangs the water. Demonstrates three effects in one readable frame.

Tips:
- Turn `debugMarkers` OFF for captures (it's for testing).
- Bump `masterIntensity` to ~1.3 for stills so effects read at a distance; keep 1.0 for GIFs.
- For GIFs: 3–6 s loops, 60 fps capture → 30 fps export, under 10 MB for Modrinth.
